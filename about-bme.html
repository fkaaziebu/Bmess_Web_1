Biomedical engineering or medical engineering is the application of engineering principles and design concepts to medicine and biology for healthcare purposes (eg, diagnostic or therapeutic).
This field seeks to close the gap between engineering and medicine, combining the design and problem-solving skills of engineering with medical biological sciences to advance healthcare treatment, including diagnosis, monitoring and therapy. Biomedical engineers can work in hosptials, public health agencies, industrial laboratories and pharmaceutical firms and research institutions such as Ghana Standards Authority, Centre for Scientific Research, Food and Drugs Authority and Narcotics and Control Board.

ROLES OF BIOMEDICAL ENGINEERS

  • Design equipments and devices such as internal organs, replacement for body parts, diagnostic machines etc.
  • Install, maintain, repair and provide technical support fo biomedical equipment.
  • Evaluate safety, efficiency and effectiveness of biomedical equipment. Train clinicians and patients in the use of biomedical equipment
  • Use their engineering knowledge to work with researchers in developing new medical devices, artificial organs, software etc.
  • Write technical reports, procedures, publish research findings and make presentations

MAJOR SUBDISCIPLINES

Bioinstrumentation, Biomechanics, Clinical engineering, Rehabilitation engineering, Computer modelling, Biomaterials, Tissue engineering, Bio signals and processing, Orthotics and Prosthesis as well as Robotics

Audit Committee

There shall b an audit committee which shall comprise of two members from third year and a member from the remaining classes, appointed by the class. Members should have a background in accounting.

Functions

  • They will audit the finances and assets of the association before the end of each semester and publish a report within 14 days of completion of its work that same semester
  • No member of the audit committee while serving on the committee shall be an officer or hold any other office in the Society.
